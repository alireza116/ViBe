VibeJS — the new edit API

A channel owns both encode (data→visual) and edit (gesture→data). Interactions are declared where the channel is defined; a constraint on an edit governs that edit's channel by construction.

One bidirectional model: change-edits (drag / resize / cycle / custom), create and remove as their own edits, pick: "nearest" proximity targeting, per-edit when arbitration, and edits that self-draw their guide via guide: true. Constraints are data-layer invariants declared at feature level (constraints: []) — pure rules over the dataset, named by data field, that hold no matter which edit fired.

1 · Vertical bars — drag to set the value

y is a linear channel carrying edit: drag(). Dragging a bar sets its y.

2 · Bars — maintainSum(100) as a data invariant

The sum rule is declared at feature level (constraints: [maintainSum(...)]), so it holds for every edit, not just the drag. Bars grow until the total hits 100. The dashed line is a declarative guide.

3 · Scatter — 2D move

Both x and y carry edit: drag(), so a drag moves the point in both.

4 · One mark, three edits (the payoff)

Drag to move (x+y), Shift-drag to resize (size→mag), click to recolor (cycle the group). Three channel-scoped edits on one mark, arbitrated by when.

5 · Likert — drag a point, or click an empty slot to place one

1D categorical x on a dot resolves to a point scale (scalePoint — the dot sits on the category tick): edit: drag() snaps to the nearest category, and a mark-level edit.create() lets a click on an empty slot mint a response.

6 · Custom edit — the escape hatch

edit.custom((datum, event) => …) gets the whole datum + event and returns a new datum. Here a vertical drag sets y and stamps touched.

7 · Create — click empty space to add a point

edit.create({ defaults }) is its own declaration: a plane click inverts the pointer through the positional channels (the same inverse drag uses) and appends a datum. Created points are then draggable through the channel edits. count(max) caps how many.

8 · Nearest pick + self-drawn guide

Thin bars are hard to hit, so the y edit uses pick: "nearest" — grab a bar from anywhere in its column. guide: true makes the edit draw its own snap ring plus the clamp bounds for its channel (read from the feature's data invariants).

9 · unique — one bar per category

create mints a bar wherever you click, but the unique({ field: "x" }) data invariant forbids two bars in the same band slot: a click on an occupied category is rejected, an empty one is filled.

10 · remove — Alt-click to delete

remove() is just another edit. Here click recolours (cycle) and Alt-click deletes — the two share the click gesture, arbitrated by when (noAlt vs alt). drag still moves.
