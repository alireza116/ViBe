# Face (emotion)

<Lead>`face` encodes emotion — a datum's fields — into a face, and lets you **directly manipulate the features** to write them back: grab the mouth and pull it into a smile, drag an eye wider, tilt a brow. A face has seven parameters; each is only editable when you **bind it to a field**, so you turn on exactly the expressions you want to elicit.</Lead>

<ApiReference entries={api} />

<Section id="emotion" title="One face — drag to feel">

The emotion preset binds `mouthCurve` to `valence` and `eyeScale` to `arousal`. Grab the mouth and pull it up to smile, or drag an eye to widen it — each gesture inverts through the field's scale, and the whole face re-derives.

  title="Valence × arousal"
  blurb="The two-field emotion preset. The mouth and eyes are the drag targets themselves."
  try={(
    <>
      <b>Drag the mouth</b> up/down (valence) or <b>drag an eye</b> up/down (arousal).
    </>
  )}
/>

</Section>

<Section id="expressive" title="Turn on more expressions">

Bind any subset of the seven params in `channels`, each as a plain `{ field }` — the binding both drives the geometry and makes that feature draggable. Unbound params stay neutral. Here every param is live: mouth (↕ curve, ↔ smirk), eyes (↕ aperture), the eyelid dot (↓ squint), the lip dot (↓ open), and the brows (↕ height, ↔ tilt).

<Example
  code={fullChernoffFace}
  title="Full Chernoff face"
  blurb="All seven parameters bound to fields; grab any feature to sculpt the expression."
  try={(
    <>
      <b>Drag</b> the mouth, eyes, brows, or the eyelid / lip dots.
    </>
  )}
/>

</Section>

<Section id="multiples" title="Many faces in a plot">

Give the face an `x` (or `y`) channel and it is placed like a `point` — one editable face per row. Set `x: { field: 'valence' }, y: { field: 'arousal' }` instead and the faces become an emotion-space scatter that moves as you edit them.
